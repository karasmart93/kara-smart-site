🌺 New Client Onboarding
Welcome to Bloom
We’re honored you’re here.
This short onboarding takes about 10 minutes. It helps us make your first session as comfortable and meaningful as possible. Take your time — there are no wrong answers.

Your First Appointment, Step by Step

  • 1

    Arrive a few minutes early

    We recommend arriving 5–10 minutes before your appointment. Our reception team will warmly welcome you and get you settled in our waiting area.

  • 2

    Meet your therapist

    Your therapist will come to greet you personally. The first session is a conversation — not a test. There’s no pressure to share more than you’re comfortable with.

  • 3

    Talk about what brings you in

    Your therapist will ask some questions to understand your goals and background. This helps them tailor their approach specifically for you.

  • 4

    Discuss next steps together

    At the end of your session, you’ll talk about what ongoing support might look like — there’s never any obligation to commit to anything on the spot.

A Note Before We Begin

Everything you share with us is confidential. The information in this onboarding goes directly to your therapist to help them prepare — it is never shared with anyone outside your care team.

If at any point you feel unsure about a question, feel free to skip it or write “prefer to discuss in session.”

Policies & Cancellation

A few important things to know before your first visit. We want everything to be clear so there are no surprises.

Our Policies

Cancellation & Rescheduling

We ask for at least 24 hours’ notice to cancel or reschedule. Cancellations with less than 24 hours’ notice may incur a late cancellation fee of $75. We understand that emergencies happen — please reach out and we’ll do our best to work with you.

Session Length & Fees

Standard sessions are 50 minutes. Fees vary by therapist and service type. Your specific fee and any insurance information will be confirmed by our office team before your first appointment.

Confidentiality

Everything discussed in your sessions is strictly confidential. The only exceptions are required by law: if there is an imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, or in cases of suspected abuse. Your therapist will discuss this with you in your first session.

Between Sessions

Your therapist is not available for crisis support between sessions. If you experience a mental health emergency, please call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room.

Acknowledgment

By continuing, you confirm that you have read and understood the policies above.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions new clients ask most often. If something isn’t covered here, please reach out — we’re happy to help.

What should I bring to my first appointment? ▼
Just yourself. If you have insurance information or a referral letter, bring those along, but don’t stress if you don’t. Our team can sort out the logistics before or after your session.
How do I know if therapy is working? ▼
Progress in therapy is rarely linear. Some people feel better quickly; for others it takes more time. Your therapist will regularly check in with you about how things are going and adjust their approach based on your feedback. The most important thing is open communication.
What if I don’t click with my therapist? ▼
The therapeutic relationship matters enormously, and it’s okay if a particular therapist isn’t the right fit. Please let us know — we’ll work with you to find someone who is a better match. There is no awkwardness in asking for this.
How often will we meet? ▼
Most clients start with weekly sessions, especially early in the process. As you make progress, sessions may shift to every two weeks or monthly. This is always a collaborative decision between you and your therapist.
Is everything I say really confidential? ▼
Yes — with very limited legal exceptions, everything you share is completely confidential. Your therapist will go over those exceptions in detail during your first session so you feel fully informed.
Can I use my insurance? ▼
Many of our therapists accept insurance, and we also offer private pay options. Our office team will confirm your specific coverage and out-of-pocket costs before your first appointment so there are no surprises.
