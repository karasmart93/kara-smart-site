The Optum Transformation

How I reduced out-of-scope work by 85% through agile transformation at Optum

The Problem
Optum’s L&D team was drowning in ad-hoc requests, unclear intake processes, and constant context-switching. Projects routinely ran over budget. Designers were stressed and burning out. Stakeholders were frustrated by slow turnaround times and inconsistent quality. The team had the talent but lacked the operational framework to scale.
The Solution
I designed, trained, and led a complete agile transformation for the team — implementing Scrum ceremonies, redesigning the intake system from scratch, creating transparent capacity planning tools, and coaching team members on sustainable workflows. This wasn’t just process documentation; it was hands-on change management with real buy-in.
The Impact
Within months, out-of-scope requests dropped by 85%. Project stress was cut in half based on team surveys. Delivery timelines became predictable. The team transformed from reactive firefighting to proactive, scalable operations. Leadership got visibility. Designers got breathing room. Stakeholders got better results.
